**System Override**

Our robotics journey began in 6th grade through EV3 classes, where an early interest in robotics evolved into a effort to design and build autonomous robots. This shared passion led to the formation of System Override, a team focused on innovation, collaboration, and technical excellence.

**Innovation**

The team focuses on developing custom solutions to enhance robot performance. This includes the use of 3D printing for rapid prototyping and the creation of custom silicone wheels to improve traction and control. The integration of hardware such as RaspberryPico-based systems and vision modules further strengthens the robot’s capabilities beyond standard platforms.

**Sustainability**

System Override adopts a resource-efficient approach by reusing components from previous projects, including wires, wheels, screws, sensors, and structural elements dating back to 2022. This practice reduces material waste while encouraging practical and creative engineering solutions.

**Open Source Initiative (RoboCup 2026)**

The team is currently developing a fully open-source robotic platform for RoboCup 2026 Internationals, transitioning beyond EV3 to build a more scalable and high-performance system. This initiative aims to share designs, code, and technical knowledge with the global robotics community, promoting accessibility and collaborative innovation.
